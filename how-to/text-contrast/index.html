Text contrast

Cavepaint always uses its palette version of white and black for coloring text.

Text contrast rules are honored for .light and .dark composable classes, so those may sometimes change the color of the text.

By defining only background colors through classes, you never have to worry about finding the correctly contrastful text color in Cavepaint. Define its background color and the rest is taken care of.

(Just in case, there's .tint and .shade if you do need to override with white text or black text, respectively.)

The technical nitty-gritty

Cavepaint prefers to use white text. If a background is dark enough to allow for a WCAG Level-I standard contrast ratio of 3.0, Cavepaint will use white text for that background color.

Otherwise, Cavepaint always shades text.

A contrast ratio of 3 may seem low, but fot tinted (white) text it works better than shaded (dark) text.

Because Cavepaint uses the palette white and black, the text color will be affected by the ambient "lighting" (really, it's just color added to every generated color) and therefore the base color.

Cavepaint's Less version can meet any WCAG with a few extra guards and the .set-contrast-ratio() mixin.
